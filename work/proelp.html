ProElp Graphics, UI/UX Designer
( Dec. 2015 - Mar. 2016 )

ProElp aims to remove the hassles and limitations of physical medical consultations. They are trying to bring all aspects of doctor consultations online, effectively minimizing the requirement of meeting a doctor in person.

It was my first internship as graphics, UI/UX designer. I joined the team in December 2015. That time it was an early startup which was growing it's branches and roots in the environment. First thing they asked me to make a logo as they wanted to launch the site ASAP.

I took out pen and paper and started working on that. I send them some designs but they finalize one and wanted to improvise it.

They selected this puzzle one as its final one and wanted to make some alterations on that so that the first and last 'p' will be easily readable. Tried many things and then finalize this one. Had some color fight but then the final match was made using these two colors and today the big logo looks like this

It cost me 4 days and I was working with home. In the first week of january I visited the office for the first time. Too much positive energy was there, everyone was motivated to make this dream come true by giving the proper commitment and time. After getting introduced to every member of the team I started with the then required onboarding screens' graphics. I spent 5 hours in the office and prepared the sketches for the oboarding screens and after getting it finalized I started with the visual part using photoshop and illustrator. There were some last moment changes but those were done and screens were changed

Now all the images can be seen on the app and the screenshots available on the playstore.

Next step was to redesign the whole website of the proelp. Challenge was to retain the whole data that was available and to redesign the whole thing that it can pass the message and idea in more persuasive way. Did some research for it as it was officially my first UX project. Made some ideas on the pages and from the first point, started with the photoshop.
